# Order Fulfillment Delay Analysis

## Executive Summary
This project analyzes delivery performance in an ecommerce business to identify late delivery patterns and potential operational issues.  
The analysis shows that while most orders are delivered on time, a meaningful portion of deliveries are delayed, particularly during specific peak periods.

---

## Business Context
This project simulates the work of a Business Analyst supporting an ecommerce company to evaluate operational performance, specifically focusing on delivery reliability.

---

## Business Problem
The business wants to understand:
- how often deliveries are late
- when late deliveries are most common
- whether delivery performance is consistent
- what actions can improve delivery reliability

---

## Stakeholders

- **Operations Team** → responsible for fulfillment and delivery performance  
- **Logistics Team** → manages shipping and delivery timelines  
- **Customer Experience Team** → handles complaints and satisfaction  
- **Business Leadership** → monitors operational KPIs and performance  

---

## Key Business Questions

- What percentage of orders are delivered late?
- How many orders are delivered on time vs late?
- Which months have the highest late delivery counts?
- What operational insights can be derived from delivery patterns?

---

## Tools Used

- Excel  
- Pivot Tables  
- Data Cleaning and Transformation  

---

## Dataset

- Brazilian Ecommerce Dataset (Olist)

---

## Key Metrics

- **Total Orders:** 99,441  
- **Late Orders:** 7,827  
- **On Time Orders:** 88,649  
- **No Delivery Data:** 2,965  
- **Late Delivery Rate:** 7.87%  

---

## Key Insights

- Late deliveries were not evenly distributed across time and peaked during November 2017, February 2018, and March 2018.
- This pattern suggests that delivery performance may be impacted during higher-demand periods, potentially due to increased operational pressure.
- While most deliveries are completed on time, a late delivery rate of 7.87% represents a meaningful operational issue that could affect customer experience.

---

## Recommendations

- Investigate operational capacity during peak demand periods to identify potential bottlenecks in fulfillment and delivery.
- Improve planning and coordination between warehouse operations and logistics providers during high-volume periods.
- Monitor late delivery rates as a key operational KPI and set thresholds for acceptable performance levels.

---

## Business Impact

Delivery delays can negatively impact customer satisfaction, increase complaints, and reduce trust in the ecommerce experience.  
Improving delivery reliability can support better customer retention and operational efficiency.

---

## What I Would Do Next as a Business Analyst

- Analyze delivery performance by region or location to identify geographic issues  
- Investigate the relationship between order volume and delivery delays  
- Evaluate logistics provider performance to identify underperforming partners  
- Build a delivery performance dashboard for ongoing monitoring
